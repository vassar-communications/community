Arts

  • Friday, August 11 12:00 pm
    “A Neoclassical Portrait of a Classicist: Houdon's Bust of Jean-Jacques Barthélemy”
    The Frances Lehman Loeb Art Center

    This exhibition offers a glimpse into how the medium used to function in the lives of amateur photographers. Comprised of 200 small black-and-white photographs mostly of American women from the early and mid-twentieth century, the photos in this exhibition were selected from a gift of more than 500 donated by Peter J. Cohen in 2015.

  • Thursday, September 7 5:30 pm
    Art Center Exhibition Lecture and Reception: Joel Smith, “How Snapshots Matter”
    Taylor Hall, room 102

    Joel Smith, Richard L. Menschel Curator of Photography, The Morgan Library & Museum, will present this lecture in connection with the exhibition Other People’s Pictures: Snapshots from the Peter J. Cohen Gift. Mr. Smith received his PhD from Princeton in 2001. From 1999 to 2005, he was Fisher Curator at the Frances Lehman Loeb Art Center and from 2005 to 2012 he was the curator of photography at the Princeton University Art Museum. Among his books are Edward Steichen: The Early Years, Steinberg at the New Yorker, and The Life and Death of Buildings: On Photography and Time. A reception and exhibition viewing in the Art Center will follow the lecture.

Upcoming

  • Wednesday, October 45:30 pm
    Lecture: Ellen Kraly, “The Search for Safe Haven in Utica, NY: U.S. Refugee Policy & Resettlement in a Global Context”
    Rockefeller Hall, 200

    Ellen Kraly—Department of Geography, Colgate University—considers U.S. refugee resettlement in Utica, NY. She will also place national refugee policy within the global context of trends and patterns of forced migration in regions of the world. The New York Declaration for Refugees and Migrants, adopted by Member States of the U.N., provides an additional touchstone for considering local, national, and global dimensions of migration and human security in this century. Sponsored by Vassar's Earth Science & Geography Department.

Accessibility

Vassar College strives to make its events, performances, and facilities accessible to all.
